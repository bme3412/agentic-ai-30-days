The ReAct Pattern

Reasoning + Acting: Interleaving thoughts with tool use

What This Demo Teaches

ReAct (Reasoning + Acting) is the pattern that makes AI agents transparent and debuggable. Instead of jumping straight to actions, the model first explains its reasoning, then acts, then interprets the results. This demo lets you see the pattern in action and understand why it matters.

💭

Thought

The model explains what it's trying to figure out and why. This creates a traceable reasoning chain you can debug.

⚙

Action

Based on its reasoning, the model decides what tool to call. The action is purposeful, not random.

👁

Observation

Tool results are fed back to the model. It interprets them in context and decides what to do next.

Key insight: ReAct creates grounded reasoning—conclusions based on actual observations, not just the model's training data. This dramatically reduces hallucination and improves reliability.

ReAct vs. Other Patterns

See how different patterns approach the same problem.

Chain-of-Thought (CoT)

Thought: Tokyo is Japan's capital. Japan has about 125 million people. Tokyo probably has around 14 million...
Answer: About 14 million
Problem: Reasoning from training data. May be outdated or wrong.

Action-Only

Action: search("Tokyo population")
Observation: 13.96 million
Answer: 13.96 million
Problem: No reasoning visible. Hard to debug if wrong.

ReAct

Thought: I need current data on Tokyo's population. Let me search.
Action: search("Tokyo population 2024")
Observation: 13.96 million (2024 estimate)
Thought: Now I can calculate 25% of 13.96 million.
Action: calculate("13960000 * 0.25")
Observation: 3490000
Thought: I have the answer grounded in real data.
Answer: 3.49 million (25% of Tokyo's 13.96 million population)
Advantage: Grounded in observations. Traceable reasoning. Self-documenting.
