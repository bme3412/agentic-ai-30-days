CrewAI Multi-Agent Systems

Orchestrating specialized agents that collaborate on complex tasks

What This Demo Teaches

CrewAI enables you to build teams of AI agents that work together, each with a distinct role. Instead of one agent doing everything, specialized agents handle what they're best at and pass results to the next agent in the workflow.

👤

Agents

Team members with specific roles, goals, backstories, and tools. Each agent focuses on what it does best.

📋

Tasks

Specific work assignments with clear deliverables. Tasks can depend on each other through context.

👥

Crews

The orchestrator that coordinates agents and tasks, manages execution, and produces the final output.

Key insight: Multi-agent systems excel when tasks require diverse expertise. A researcher finds information, an analyst interprets it, a writer crafts the output—each agent's focused role produces better results than one agent attempting everything.

How Context Flows Between Tasks

See how each task's output becomes the next task's input.

Task 1: Research 🔍 Researcher
Input: "Research the future of renewable energy"
Output: 
Solar capacity grew 25% YoY globally. Wind energy now
provides 7% of global electricity. Battery storage
costs dropped 89% since 2010. Key players: Tesla,
NextEra, Vestas. Emerging: green hydrogen, offshore
wind, perovskite solar cells.
↓ context=[research_task]
Task 2: Analysis 📈 Analyst
Input: Research output + "Analyze trends and identify key insights"
Output: 
Key Trends:
1. Cost parity reached - renewables now cheapest source
2. Storage solving intermittency - enables 24/7 clean power
3. Policy acceleration - IRA driving US investment

Investment Implications:
- Solar + storage companies well-positioned
- Fossil fuel stranded asset risk increasing
- Grid infrastructure needs $4T investment by 2030
↓ context=[research_task, analysis_task]
Task 3: Writing ✍ Writer
Input: Research + Analysis + "Write executive summary"
Output: 
EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: The Future of Renewable Energy

The renewable energy sector has reached an inflection
point. With solar growing 25% annually and battery
costs down 89% since 2010, clean energy is now the
cheapest option in most markets.

Three trends will shape the next decade:
1. Cost leadership driving adoption
2. Storage enabling grid reliability
3. Policy support accelerating investment

Organizations should evaluate their energy strategy now
as the transition accelerates faster than most forecasts.
